Biography

I am a fourth year PhD candidate in Dr. Suzanne Alonzo’s lab in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of California, Santa Cruz. I am interested in the coevolution of females and males using a combination of empirical work and mathematical modeling. For my dissertation, I am researching how females influence the evolution of male behavior via cryptic female choice (females bias fertilization to specific males) and how cryptic female choice affects speciation. I am also an independent R Shiny web app developer.

Any opinion, findings, and conclusions or recommendations expressed in material on this website are those of my own and do not necessarily reflect the views of the National Science Foundation.
